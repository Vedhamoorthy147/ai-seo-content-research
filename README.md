AI-Powered SEO Content Production – Research

I chose this topic because AI is changing the way content is created, but there are a lot of different opinions about how it should actually be used. Some people believe AI can handle most of the content production process, while others think it should only be used for research and planning.

I wanted to learn from people who are actively working in SEO and content marketing rather than relying on general articles or tool documentation. My goal is to understand what is working in practice and what challenges people are facing when using AI for content production.

What's in this Repository

- "/research/sources.md" – List of the experts selected for this research along with notes on why they were chosen.
- "/research/linkedin-posts/" – Recent LinkedIn posts collected and organized by expert.
- "/research/youtube-transcripts/" – Video transcripts and notes from relevant YouTube content.
- "/research/other/" – Additional resources such as articles, newsletters, and research reports.

How I Selected the Experts

I focused on people who regularly share practical SEO and content marketing insights. Instead of selecting experts based only on follower count, I looked for individuals who discuss real-world strategies, experiments, case studies, and lessons from their own work.

Some experts were already familiar to me, while others were discovered through recommendations, industry discussions, and recurring mentions in SEO communities.

Research Goals

Through this project, I want to better understand:

- How AI is being used in content creation workflows.
- The role of AI in research, planning, writing, and optimization.
- Whether topical authority strategies still work when content is produced with AI assistance.
- Common mistakes and challenges that teams face when scaling AI-generated content.
- The balance between automation and human expertise in modern SEO.

My intention is to use this research as the foundation for a future playbook that summarizes practical approaches, common patterns, and lessons shared by experienced practitioners.
